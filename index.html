Cuckoo Attack Returning in Breath of the Wild

  • A returning feature in The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild comes in the form of the cuckoos attacking Link. This is a hidden mechanic in most (if not all ) Zelda games where if the player does enough damage to the chickens (cuckoos), an event is triggered which causes many Cuckoos to fly down from the sky and attack the player.

Secret Mechanic in Main-line Pokemon Games

  • There is a hidden mechanic in the mainline pokemon games that not many players actually know about. This can be done by simply standing in (or near) tall grass in the overworld, going into your pokemon menu, and selecting 'cut' from a pokemon that knows the Hidden Move.
  • If you don't have the Cut HM you can find it from the Cut Masters house to the left of the Pokemon Center in Rustboro City

10 Hidden Secrets in Super Mario Odyssey.

  • The secret painting in the Sand Kingdom: In the Sand Kingdom, there is a secret painting hidden in a small alcove near the Jaxi statue. It can be tricky to spot, and getting to it requires some clever platforming.
  • The hidden room in the Ruined Kingdom: In the Ruined Kingdom, there is a hidden room behind a cracked wall near the top of the main tower. Getting there requires precise jumping and careful exploration.
  • The invisible platforms in the Luncheon Kingdom: In the Luncheon Kingdom, there are invisible platforms that can be used to reach a secret area. They can be hard to spot, and navigating them requires careful jumps and timing.
  • The hidden moon in the Cloud Kingdom: In the Cloud Kingdom, there is a hidden moon that can be reached by finding a secret path off the main platform. The path is well-hidden and requires some exploration to find.
  • The secret island in the Seaside Kingdom: In the Seaside Kingdom, there is a secret island that can only be reached by using a specific capture ability. Figuring out which enemy to capture and how to use its abilities can be a challenge.
  • The secret boss fight in the Metro Kingdom: In the Metro Kingdom, there is a secret boss fight that can only be accessed by completing a specific challenge. The challenge itself is difficult, and the boss fight is even tougher.
  • The hidden music notes in the Wooded Kingdom: In the Wooded Kingdom, there are hidden music notes that must be collected to unlock a Power Moon. The notes are well-hidden, and some require tricky platforming to reach.
  • The secret art exhibit in the Cap Kingdom: In the Cap Kingdom, there is a secret art exhibit that can only be accessed by solving a puzzle. The puzzle is challenging, and the exhibit itself contains hidden details that are easy to miss.
  • The hidden platform in the Snow Kingdom: In the Snow Kingdom, there is a hidden platform that can only be reached by using a specific capture ability. Figuring out how to use the ability and where to find the platform requires careful exploration.
  • The secret room in the Mushroom Kingdom: In the Mushroom Kingdom, there is a secret room that can only be accessed by solving a puzzle involving a painting. The puzzle is tricky, and the room contains hidden details that are easy to miss.

Top 5 Easter Eggs in The Legends of Zelda Ocarina of Time

  • Mario Paintings in Hyrule Castle: When you first meet Princess Zelda in the courtyard of Hyrule Castle, you can zoom in through the window on the right side and see various painting depicting Mario characters
  • Skyward Sword References: On the 3DS version of the game, there are Skyward Sword references scattered in multiple locations such as one of the rooms in Gerudo fortress and the second floor of the house at Lon Lon Ranch.
  • Talon and Malon's Neckpieces: Both Talon and Malon wear a clasp on their clothing that looks like Bowser, the main villain of the Super Mario Bros. series
  • Talon and Ingo: Ever noticed that these two characters resemble Mario and Luigi from the Super Mario Bros. series?
  • Rocket Eye Statues: If you use an explosive on one of the eye statues that tell the time, they will blast off like a rocket.

Secret Boss in Kirby's Dreamland 3


Image from Kirby Fan Wiki

Kirby's Dreamland 3 contains a hidden boss called Zero (and his Dark Matter). This boss can only be fought once Kirby has collected all of the Heart Stars. There are 30 of these in total, and Kirby can only collect them once. Be sure to grab all 6 from each level in the game and you will be able to unlock the Love-Love stick which allows you to defeat Zero's Dark Matter. In short, this boss is unlocked by collecting all of the Heart Stars in the Game, it is the true final boss of Kirby's Dreamland 3.
